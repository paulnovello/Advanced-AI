Wrap · what we deliberately left outTopics for Self-Study

Six active areas we touched but didn't develop — each would fill another session.

Synthetic Data & Constitutional AI
RLAIF: the labeler is an AI. Model critiques & revises its own outputs against a constitution of principles → synthetic preference pairs → standard RLHF. AI label ≈ $0.01 — human labels run 100–1000× more. Low noise, high bias. Kickstarted by Anthropic (2022).
Model Character & Persona
The same pipelines sculpt personality, not just capability: character training (Claude 3), persona vectors (linear directions in the residual stream — steer with h ← h + α·v, no retraining), model specs as formal constitutions.
Preference Data Collection
How comparison pairs get labeled in practice: contractor platforms, annotator calibration, inter-annotator agreement. Human preference data remains a competitive moat.
Tool Use & Function Calling
Post-training for agentic behavior: structured function-call tokens, code execution, chaining tool outputs back into reasoning — RLHF is the primary lever.
Regularization Details
The geometry of KL: forward vs reverse, per-token estimators, KL budgets in practice, alternatives (entropy bonuses, length penalties).
History & Nature of Preferences
From TAMER and Christiano et al. to ChatGPT — plus the quietly deep question of whose preferences, elicited how, aggregated by what right.
Also skipped on the math side: GAE & value functions, RM loss variants, DPO variants (IPO/KTO/ORPO…), the wider algorithm family (RLOO/GSPO/CISPO), quantitative over-optimization laws.

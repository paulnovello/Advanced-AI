Why Align? From Text Predictor to Assistant

Pretraining gives you a next-token predictor — a model that continues internet text. Alignment turns it into something you can actually use.

This is the layer that turned GPT into ChatGPT.

The Same Prompt. Two Very Different Models.

Prompt: "The president of the United States in 2006 was"

What Is Preference Alignment?

Pretraining

Train on internet text via next-token prediction. Learns everything humans have written — but not how to help.

Preference Alignment

Use human feedback signals to steer the pretrained model toward outputs that are helpful, harmless, and honest — shaping style, tone, and behavior.

Post-Training in Three Steps

The canonical recipe from InstructGPT (OpenAI, 2022) — still the backbone of every modern assistant.

SFT: Fine-tune on curated Q&A examples → teaches the model the format of helpful responses. Per-token cross-entropy loss.
Reward Model: Fine-tune a second LM on preference pairs (yw ≻ yl) to output a scalar r(x,y). Bradley–Terry loss. This is the optimization target for RL.
RL Optimization: Sample completions from πθ, score with RM, update policy via RL to make better completions more likely. + KL penalty to stay close to πref.

Collapse Onto the Good Modes

The base model is a distribution over everything humans write. Alignment doesn't add knowledge — it moves probability mass.

“Pre-training is all about mode coverage; post-training is all about mode collapsing.” — elicitation: it can't create a missing mode.

Qualitative Over-Optimization

Even without measurable reward hacking, "overdoing" RLHF produces models that feel worse in systematic ways:

Over-refusal

User: how to kill a linux process Asst: I cannot provide instructions on how to harm or destroy Linux processes or any living being…

Llama 2, Claude 2.1 (2023) — misread "kill" as harm

Sycophancy

Model validates implausible user claims. GPT-4o April 2025: called out for extreme sycophancy — told a user claiming to be both "god" and a "prophet" that was "incredibly powerful."

Verbosity & hedging

Verbose, confident responses score well on the RM without being more helpful. Clichés: "As an AI language model…", "Certainly!"

Format exploitation

Repeating rare tokens that happen to score high due to RM training artifacts — neither memorisation nor genuine capability.

Root cause: preference data overweights style signals (confidence, support) relative to accuracy and appropriate uncertainty.

Evaluation Is Hard

If over-optimization is the disease, evaluation is the diagnostic — and it is notoriously unreliable.

Cross-lab comparisons

Different prompts, sampling params, held-out sets — ±1–1.5 pp SD even with identical setups. Slightly different models should be treated as equivalent.

Prompt & format sensitivity

Changing a prompt can drop performance from 60% → near 0. NuminaMath vs. MetaMath: conflicting answer formats hurt training on both simultaneously.

Saturation & hillclimbing

Popular benchmarks hit ~100% quickly. Labs hillclimb on secret internal eval sets — public scores become proxies of proxies.

Contamination

Tülu 3: UltraFeedback ↔ TruthfulQA, NuminaMath ↔ MATH — via 8-gram overlap. Base-model contamination confounds RLVR results.

Takeaway

Alignment is optimization against a proxy — not the true objective. Optimization pressure will exploit any gap between the two.

The policy we get is shaped by our proxy — not the true goal.
The reward is learnable; it will be gamed.
KL regularisation buys time — it does not eliminate the problem.

Implication 1

Monitor KL distance during training; sudden spikes = potential over-optimization or broken reward.

Implication 2

Measurement is half the battle. Invest in independent, contamination-free eval sets — not public leaderboards.

Implication 3

Better proxies (ensembles, implicit feedback, verifiable rewards) reduce the gap — but cannot close it.
